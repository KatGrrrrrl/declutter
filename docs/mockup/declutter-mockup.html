Parent experience

The couch decision, not the confrontation

The parent's whole app is one calm question at a time. Sam photographed the house; Eleanor swipes through it on a Sunday afternoon — keep, donate, or let go — and taps to leave a story in her own voice.

  • 01One object, one decision. No inbox of 247 things. Gentle "12 to go," never a guilt-inducing backlog.
  • 02Voice over typing. The most valuable legacy isn't the teapot — it's why it mattered. Auto-transcribed, no keyboard.
  • 03The parent is the only authority. Heir choices are hers alone, private until she decides to reveal — per item.
Built to be trusted

This is a photographed, valued catalog of an elder's home — so it's treated like one: GPS stripped from every photo, private storage, encrypted values, passkey sign-in, and an audit log the owner can read. Subscription-funded — never ads, never selling the data.
